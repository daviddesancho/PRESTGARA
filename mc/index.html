Monte Carlo simulation of an Ising model

The Ising model is very useful as a model for magnetization that will allow for exploring the Monte Carlo method. In the Ising model we consider a two-dimensional lattice of particles, each of which has only two possible spins, "up" and "down". In our particular example we will consider that interactions are limited to nearest neighbours. A parallel pair results in a lower energy than an antiparallel pair. Ideally, we would also consider the effect of an external field on the results, but today we will focus on the effects of the temperature only.

For this exercise we use the code developed by Frans Pretorius and William East (Princeton University). The first thing we need to do is download two pieces of Python code: ising_MHMC.py and ising.py.
